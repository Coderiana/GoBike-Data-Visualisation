Ford GoBike Analysis¶

by Adriana Zurawska¶

Investigation Overview¶

In my presentation I will discuss the Ford GoBike user population and its behaviour in connection to the service development over time. The most interesting aspects in my analysis were: user demographics and user behavior. In my presentation I will focus on discussing the user profile - who is using GoBike and how this can be used for the further development of the service.

Dataset Overview¶

The dataset consists of GoBike ride data collected over 18 months. The dataset holds data on bike id, user birth year, gender, journey start point, journey end point, start and end time and date of the trip.

When the trips are longest ?¶

Duration of the ride can help to determine how the bikes are used.

The longest rides take place on Thursdays and Fridays. This is an interesting observation and maybe it is connected with more traffic on those days or some other events that can influence the speed of the ride.

When the bikes are used?¶

The most busy time of the day is in the morning between 7 and 9 am and by the evening between 5 and 7 pm. This points out towards the cycle-to-work sheme and it is evident that majority of users is taking the bike to commute to and from work. This is a great find that GoBike is used to help professionals commute to work. This user group seems to be the root of the service, not the leisure bikers.

How old are users?¶

The average user age is 35.47 and majority of the users are between 27 and 41 years old (green box). The boxplot whiskers are showing the minimum and maximum user age values, while the black dots are the outlying points outside the ususal data. The most active age group consists of young adults and professionals.

Diving into more detail in regard to user age distribution, I wan't to discuss the user age groups egnagement over time.

When the GoBike has launched in June 2017 the most active age group was of users between 30-40 years old. This trend has changed exaxtly year after, in July 2018. Since then, the most widely represented age group was of users who are between 20-30 years old. There are seasonal changes visible in all age groups, so in this case I think it is best to compare the same months in 2017 in 2018 with each other. Surprisingly in October 2017 and 2018 all age groups showed increase in numbers, even though it would be expected to be rather cold for the bike trips.

User type and weekday trend¶

There seem to be significantly less Subscribers towards end of the week: in Thursday and Friday, and the most Customers made trips on Thursday. This is surprising discovery as I would assume that there are more Customers during weekends (weekend trips with kids, cycling with friends) when on weekdays I would expect the same high amount of Subscribers throughout the week (cycling to work).
